Cancer Statistics Visualizations

Cancer is a group of diseases involving abnormal cell growth with the potential to invade or spread to other parts of the body. These contrast with benign tumors, which do not spread.

Type

Deaths by Cancer Type in the World

Tracheal, bronchus, and lung cancer has the highest number of deaths, with 2020 deaths per 100,000 population. Following this, colon and rectum cancer is the second highest, accounting for 1040 deaths per 100,000. Stomach cancer is the third most deadly, with 954 deaths per 100,000 population.

Survival Rate of Cancer in Different Stages

In the early stage, female breast cancer shows the highest survival rate at 88% , while lung, trachea, and bronchial cancer have the lowest at 37%. By stage 4, lung, trachea, and bronchial cancer still exhibit the lowest survival rate at just 6% . The survival rates decline significantly across all types, with female breast cancer dropping to 23% and nasopharyngeal cancer plummeting from 64% to a mere 2%.

Country

Cancer Rates and Mortality Rates by Gender

The highest cancer rates for both males and females are found in Australia , with rates of 514.3 for males and 415.2 for females. However, the highest cancer mortality rates for both genders are in Mongolia, with rates of 227.5 for males and 145.5 for females.

Death Rate from Cancer (2010 to 2021)

The death rate from cancer has decreased significantly from 2010 to 2021, indicating improvements in treatment and prevention strategies. This trend reflects the collective efforts in public health initiatives aimed at reducing cancer mortality rates globally.
